# Phase 9B Sample Minion Illustrations v0.1

This set completes the six missing minion portraits from the 12-card Phase 9B
sample. The existing eight frozen archetype-anchor illustrations are used only
as visual references; these are new subjects rather than edits.

## Shared production constraints

Use case: stylized-concept

Asset type: production-direction candidate for a 2:3 portrait game-card
illustration, artwork only.

Style/medium: warm hand-painted Wandering Storybook watercolor with dark
walnut/colored ink on fibrous ivory paper; visible grain, irregular brush
edges, broad readable shapes, restrained detail, semi-realistic anatomy.
Warm and adventurous, not grimdark, not anime/gacha, not glossy digital
collectible-card fantasy, not photorealistic, and not childish mascot art.

Composition: one dominant subject; the face and category-defining large shape
remain in the central upper 65–68% and readable at 160×240. Background stays
one to two contrast steps below the subject. Leave the outer 7% free of
critical details.

Constraints: artwork only; absolutely no card frame, text, letters, numbers,
readable writing, icons, emblems, badges, UI, labels, borders, watermark,
signature, or title ribbon.

## Forge Soul species constraint

Forge Souls are non-human living forged shells animated by furnace spirits.
The armor is the body; there is no wearer inside. Show hollow cavities,
disconnected broad iron plates held by restrained ember-colored soul
filaments, furnace grilles, three-pronged forged clamps, and wedge-shaped
supports.

Never show a human face, skin, hair, ears, eyes, flesh, five-fingered hands,
human boots, a waist/pelvis structure, cloth clothing, knight, paladin,
blacksmith, or person in armor. Also avoid sci-fi robotics, wires, pistons,
exposed gears, circuitry, steampunk clutter, skulls, demons, horns, gothic
spikes, and excessive fire.

## 1. 回火修补匠 / Tempering Mender

Reference roles:

- Image 1: Forge Soul species and material-language reference — non-human,
  hollow blackened-iron shell, broad plates, restrained ember spirit.
- Image 2: Forge Soul silhouette and visual-authority reference — heavy forged
  geometry, furnace motif, readable shapes.
- Image 3: overall Wandering Storybook medium, palette, paper grain, and
  card-scale composition reference.

Create a new medium-small Tier 2 Forge Soul repairer, clearly less massive than
the furnace king. Its asymmetrical body is made from hinged charcoal iron
plates around a hollow furnace cowl and a small recessed kiln core. One
three-pronged forged clamp holds a short cold-steel tempering paddle; the
other steadies one detached chamfered shield plate on a low wooden repair
trestle. A broad semicircular repair clamp and the shield plate form the two
largest readable shapes. Only a few tiny embers and one soft molten-orange
seam indicate active repair. Warm caravan repair bay at dusk, very simple and
low contrast. No additional character, no human blacksmith, no apron, no
hammer pose, no face, no five-fingered hands, no gears, no spark shower.

## 2. 裂甲复仇者 / Cracked-Armor Avenger

Reference roles:

- Image 1: Forge Soul species and material-language reference.
- Image 2: Forge Soul rank, mass, and monarchic silhouette reference.
- Image 3: overall Wandering Storybook medium, palette, paper grain, and
  card-scale composition reference.

Create a new Tier 4 Forge Soul avenger with a forceful forward stance. It is a
non-human hollow spirit shell assembled from asymmetrical overlapping shield
plates. One large broken shoulder-to-torso plate carries a single bold glowing
fracture; a recessed circular furnace core is visible behind it. One arm ends
in a broad integrated wedge-shaped guard made from a snapped shield edge,
never a human hand or conventional sword. The damaged outer shell is still
protective and imposing, suggesting both Shield and a death-triggered legacy.
In the distant low-contrast forge haze, include only one small unfinished
cooling plate shape as a quiet hint of a later Forge Soul reward; no floating
cards or icons. Charcoal, dark red, cold steel, restrained molten orange.
Avoid a person in armor, knight anatomy, skulls, demon traits, gothic spikes,
giant flames, sci-fi robot parts, exposed mechanisms, and visual clutter.

## 3. 腐叶承嗣 / Rotleaf Heir

Reference roles:

- Image 1: Wild Spirit anatomy and warm watercolor rendering reference.
- Image 2: Wild Spirit organic shape language, scale, and moonlit palette
  reference.
- Image 3: overall Wandering Storybook medium, paper grain, and card-scale
  composition reference.

Create a new Tier 2 Wild Spirit: a compact young forest boar guardian with
semi-realistic animal anatomy, broad front-facing shoulders, root-like hooves,
and bark plates growing naturally through russet-brown fur. A mantle of large
drooping, weathered leaves protects its neck and back; two short broken branch
shoots form a modest crown. It stands crosswise over a narrow woodland trail
in a protective Taunt posture. Among the brown and mossy decayed leaves, one
small warm-green shoot survives, quietly suggesting inherited growth after
the guardian falls. Moss green, bark brown, muted moon teal, small warm-yellow
accents. Background is a simple damp forest opening. Avoid gore, corpse
imagery, grotesque decay, mushroom overload, neon green, oversized cute eyes,
mascot proportions, and extra creatures.

## 4. 狐群巢母 / Fox-Den Matriarch

Reference roles:

- Image 1: Wild Spirit anatomy and warm watercolor rendering reference.
- Image 2: Wild Spirit organic shape language, scale, and moonlit palette
  reference.
- Image 3: overall Wandering Storybook medium, paper grain, and card-scale
  composition reference.

Create a new mature Tier 4 Wild Spirit fox matriarch, one dominant subject,
with semi-realistic vulpine anatomy. Her substantial russet body is interwoven
with bark ridges and restrained mossy leaf edges. One large sweeping tail
curls around her body to create a clear den-like arch and protective nest
silhouette. Inside that low-contrast hollow, use only a soft two-tailed shadow
shape and two tiny leaf-warm glimmers as an understated suggestion of nested
lineage; they must not compete as separate characters. Her expression is
alert, capable, and maternal rather than cute. Warm russet, bark brown, moss,
moon-teal shadow, and sparse warm-yellow light. Quiet forest den at blue hour.
Avoid a cute family portrait, visible cub lineup, cartoon proportions,
multiple glossy eyes, anime kitsune styling, shrine imagery, spectral armies,
neon effects, and particle clutter.

## 5. 秘页折光师 / Secret-Page Refractor

Reference roles:

- Image 1: Starbound artisan costume, thin open astrolabe arcs, and restrained
  observatory-light reference.
- Image 2: Starbound scholar authority, parchment/indigo palette, and open sky
  composition reference.
- Image 3: overall Wandering Storybook medium, paper grain, and card-scale
  composition reference.

Create a new Tier 3 Starbound refraction scholar, an older androgynous human
artisan distinct from the reference characters. They wear a practical layered
indigo coat with a silver-white scarf and hold an open blank parchment folio
through a thin triangular glass-prism frame. Exactly two broad, restrained
bands of pale cyan and refractive violet light cross the blank pages and form
one subtle protective translucent plane near the forearm, suggesting Shield
and the first two spells granting growth. Use delicate open brass arcs, never
a closed halo. Place the subject on a quiet open-air archive balcony at
twilight. No readable page marks, letters, runes, formulas, icons, or UI.
Avoid a floating-book swarm, battle-mage pose, giant crystal armor, dense
particles, ornate star robes, thick machinery, and a face resembling either
reference character.

## 6. 星图掮客 / Star-Map Broker

Reference roles:

- Image 1: Starbound artisan costume, thin open astrolabe arcs, and restrained
  observatory-light reference.
- Image 2: Starbound scholar authority, parchment/indigo palette, and open sky
  composition reference.
- Image 3: overall Wandering Storybook medium, paper grain, and card-scale
  composition reference.

Create a new Tier 3 Starbound broker, a mature stocky human man with a
silver-streaked short beard and a calm, shrewd expression, distinct from both
reference characters. He wears a practical deep-indigo traveling coat with a
plain parchment sash and carries a compact leather chart satchel. He presents
an open portable chart case containing layered blank parchment sheets, two
sealed chart tubes, and one thin brass measuring divider. Two offset open
crescent route frames behind the case suggest repeated searching and
selection, but never form a closed halo. The profession must read as a
well-traveled information broker, not a wizard or noble. Quiet observatory
market at blue hour, kept low contrast. No readable constellations, writing,
symbols, cards, icons, UI, abacus, royal robes, heavy machinery, or dense star
particles.
